abstract: "Despite remarkable advancements in emulating human-like behavior through
 Large Language Models (LLMs), current textual simulations do not adequately address
 the notion of time. To this end, we introduce TimeArena, a novel textual simulated
 environment that incorporates complex temporal dynamics and constraints that better 
 reflect real-life planning scenarios. In TimeArena, agents are asked to complete multiple 
 tasks as soon as possible, allowing for parallel processing to save time. We implement 
 the dependency between actions, the time duration for each action, and the occupancy of 
 the agent and the objects in the environment. TimeArena grounds to 30 real-world tasks in 
 cooking, household activities, and laboratory work. We conduct extensive experiments with 
 various state-of-the-art LLMs using TimeArena. Our findings reveal that even the most 
 powerful models, e.g., GPT-4, still lag behind humans in effective multitasking, 
 underscoring the need for enhanced temporal awareness in the development of 
 language agents."
